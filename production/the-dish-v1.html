The Dish: ForkFox's launch, explained

An iOS app that scores individual dishes — not restaurants. Live in San Francisco and Philadelphia. Here's what it is, why we built it, and how to try it.

ForkFox Team · April 18, 2026 · 6 min read

Why we built this

Five-star ratings are lies we all agree to tell. A restaurant earns 4.6 stars because the patio is nice, the owner walks the floor, and one reviewer was having a bad day. That tells you nothing about the thing you actually came to eat.

A 4.6-star restaurant can serve a 6 out of 10 biryani. The reviews never told you that — but now the algorithm does.

Frequently asked

Is ForkFox a restaurant review app?
No — ForkFox scores individual dishes, not restaurants. Two plates at the same restaurant can earn wildly different scores.
Which cities are live?
San Francisco and Philadelphia, as of April 2026. More cities after our beta period.
How do I join the beta?
Enter your email on the homepage and we'll send a TestFlight invite within 24 hours. iOS only for now.
What does a dish score mean?
Five attributes — flavor, texture, value, execution, context — combined into a 0-to-100 score personalized to your taste profile.
Is it free?
Yes. Free during beta, no ads, no platform games. We'll talk pricing openly before anything changes.
