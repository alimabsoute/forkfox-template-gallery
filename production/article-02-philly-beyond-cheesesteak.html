What Philly eats when it's not eating a cheesesteak

Beyond Pat's and Geno's, there's a city most tourists miss. The BYOB underground. The Italian Market's Vietnamese corner. Baltimore Ave's Ethiopian stretch. Here's where locals eat.

Ask a Philly tourist where to eat and they'll give you two words: Pat's, or Geno's. Ask a Philly local and they'll sigh, then start listing streets. Baltimore Ave after dark. The Italian Market at 7 a.m. A corner of Fishtown where you can't see the sign and you don't need to.

Frequently asked

Where should a first-time Philly visitor eat, not counting cheesesteaks?
Start with DiNic's roast pork at Reading Terminal, then head to the Italian Market for a Vietnamese dinner, and finish with a BYOB in Fishtown.
What's a BYOB and why does Philly have so many?
Bring-Your-Own-Bottle restaurants — no liquor license, you bring the wine. Philly's high liquor license costs made BYOB the default for smaller operators.
Is Baltimore Avenue safe at night?
The Ethiopian stretch (42nd–50th) is lively most evenings. Lively is a good word for Philly.
What's the best Vietnamese spot in the Italian Market?
Locals debate Nam Phuong vs. Pho 75 endlessly. ForkFox beta will score both plates individually once the dishes are in.
Where do Philly chefs eat on their day off?
Often the Italian Market Vietnamese spots, or the Reading Terminal's non-tourist counters. Rarely the tourist-map restaurants.
